# Case Study: The "Winning Ticket" Phishing Scam

### 1. What is Phishing?
Phishing is a type of social engineering where attackers masquerade as a trusted entity to steal sensitive information such as login credentials, credit card numbers, or personal data. It is the "digital hook" that leads to identity theft and financial loss.

### 2. The Step-by-Step Scenario (Fictional but Realistic)
* **Step 1: The Bait:** A college student receives an urgent email that appears to be from their university’s athletic department or a popular sports betting platform. The subject line reads: *"URGENT: Your Season Pass Access is Expiring – Verify Now."*
* **Step 2: The Hook:** The email contains a link to a "Verification Portal" that looks identical to the official university login page, complete with logos and branding.
* **Step 3: The Catch:** The student, fearing they will lose access to game tickets, enters their student ID and password.
* **Step 4: The Exploit:** The attacker captures these credentials in real-time. They immediately log into the student's actual account, change the recovery email, and access connected payment methods or sensitive academic records.

### 3. Who is Targeted?
While anyone can be a victim, college students are frequent targets because they often use multiple campus-linked services (WiFi, portals, meal plans) and are quick to react to time-sensitive notifications regarding sports, grades, or financial aid.

### 4. Consequences
The consequences are often severe. Beyond the immediate loss of account access, victims face potential financial fraud, identity theft, and a compromised digital reputation. For a student, this could lead to leaked private data or even the loss of academic standing if their account is used to send spam or malware to the rest of the campus.
