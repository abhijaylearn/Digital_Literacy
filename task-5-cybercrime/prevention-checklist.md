
###  General Digital Safety
1. **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA):** Secure your Google, LinkedIn, and Instagram accounts using an authenticator app or SMS codes to prevent unauthorized logins.
2. **Audit App Permissions:** Regularly check which apps have access to your camera, microphone, and contacts. Revoke access for any app that doesn't strictly need it.
3. **Use Strong, Unique Passwords:** Avoid using the same password for your university portal and your social media. Use a password manager to keep track of complex strings.
4. **Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Sensitive Tasks:** Never log into your bank account or student portal using free, unsecured Wi-Fi in cafes or transit hubs.
5. **Verify "Urgent" Requests:** If you receive a message claiming your "Scholarship" or "Placement Account" will be deleted unless you click a link, it is likely a phishing scam.
6. **Think Before You Post:** Avoid sharing your Aadhaar number, home address, or private phone number in public stories or "About" sections.

###  Financial & UPI Safety (India Specific)
7. **PIN is for SENDING, Not Receiving:** Remember that you **never** need to enter your UPI PIN to receive money. If someone sends you a "Collect Request" to give you a prize, it is a scam.
8. **Verify the Merchant Name:** Before hitting 'Pay' on GPay, PhonePe, or Paytm, always double-check the verified name displayed on the screen to ensure it matches the intended recipient.

###  How to Report Cybercrime in India
If you or someone you know falls victim to a digital scam, act immediately to increase the chances of recovering lost funds:

* **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal:** [https://cybercrime.gov.in](https://www.cybercrime.gov.in)
* **National Helpline Number:** **1930** (Available 24/7)
